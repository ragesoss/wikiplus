09 · The divider is the spine

The column-divider rule itself is part of the mark: a 3px indigo spine sits exactly on the divide, carries a small “+” flag, and continues down between the columns into the page — so the wordmark and the page structure are one object. “wiki” hangs to its left, “plus” to its right. Left (Wiki): serif “wiki” reading into the article column. Right (Plus): indigo Source Sans 900 “plus” reading into the rail. The rule that organises the whole layout becomes the logo’s backbone. Mobile (< lg): there’s no divide and no spine, so the “+” flag detaches and sits inline between the words as a small hardbox glyph: wiki + plus.
