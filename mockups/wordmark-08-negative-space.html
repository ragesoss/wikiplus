08 · Negative-space “+”

Bold and minimal: a full-height indigo Indigo Press panel butts the white serif “wiki” field on the divide, and the “+” is knocked out of the indigo as white negative space sitting right at the seam — it reads by absence, where the wiki-white shows through the plus block. Left (Wiki): quiet serif on white. Right (Plus): a saturated indigo hardbox, Source Sans 900 “plus”. The knockout makes the white of Wikipedia literally form the plus mark — the two worlds defining each other. Mobile (< lg): the knockout is internal to the panel, so the lockup collapses to one inline mark; the panel’s left edge stands in for the lost seam. Accessibility: the white-on-indigo “plus” text carries the meaning (AA contrast); the knockout glyph is decorative (aria-hidden) and never the sole signal.
