wiki+plus Centered-wordmark study — straddling the wiki / plus seam

Ten standalone explorations of one fixed idea: instead of splitting the wordmark to the two outer edges, center the mark on the wiki | plus dividing line — so “wiki” reads just left of the divide (drawing from Wikipedia: Georgia serif, restrained, encyclopedic) and the “+ / plus” reads just right of it (drawing from the playful content-creator “Indigo Press” side: Source Sans 900, hardbox, indigo). The seam between them is the real 1fr | 360px column boundary — well right of page-center — and every mockup shows a sliver of the two columns beneath the header so the straddle reads. Palette is the committed identity (indigo #676EB4, violet #5248AF, teal #2A8270, action #1F6F95, ink #2C2C2C; gold deliberately unused). Each card opens a full mockup with a desktop straddle, a collapse preview, and a rationale caption.

Design exploration only — not production app code. The current shipped header lives in components/topic/TopicHeader.tsx.
