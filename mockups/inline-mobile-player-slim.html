Slim mobile player — exactly four controls

On mobile, a playing video is the frame + ONE thin control row, and nothing else — no caption, creator, description, or chips by default. The reader keeps reading the article while it plays. The four controls are Close · Move · Curate · See context. Curate and See context are single entry points that expand to reveal their content. There is no custom Maximize button (fullscreen is the embed's own native control; rotate-to-maximize stays automatic). All views are framed at true ~390px phone width; the focus ring is shown in gold for the demo only.

1 · Default slim playing — the whole chrome recommended: one row

Above-frame chrome: none. Below-frame chrome: one 46px row of four controls. At 390px the four words fit comfortably stacked under a small glyph; D ≈ frame(219) + bar(46) = 265px — a far slimmer dock than the launch baseline, leaving ~59% of the viewport (≈500px @ 850) showing the article.

4 · Maximized / landscape — no chrome button

There is no custom Maximize control in the four. Fullscreen = the embed's own native ⛶ button inside the iframe (shown faint, bottom-right of every frame above). Rotate-to-maximize stays an automatic CSS behavior (the same <section> + iframe flip to inset-0 on orientationchange — never requestFullscreen). In maximized/landscape the four-control bar is hidden while the video fills the screen; rotating back to portrait restores the slim dock.

4 · landscape (auto) — bar hidden, native ⛶ available

No wiki+ chrome over the video in landscape. The embed's native fullscreen (⛶) is the only fullscreen affordance. A thin Close remains reachable (top-left, condensed) so the reader is never stuck.

Move names the destination (recommend "Move to top" / "Move to bottom" over "Dock at…" — plainer, verb-first, matches the existing component's accessible names). The bar control reads "Curate"; the revealed direction reads "✦ Curate" (identical to desktop #123's on-card and in-player label) so the two surfaces share one vocabulary. "See context" (not "Info" / "Details") names the wiki+ value — context, the fact-vs-opinion separation — and generalizes the curated "Context ▸" expander.

Accessibility of the reveals

• Each of the four is a real <button>, ≥46px, full word + plain glyph (never the obscure ⤢/⤒, never glyph/color alone). Visible :focus-visible ring (brand; shown gold here for the demo).
• Curate / See context carry aria-expanded + aria-controls pointing at their panel; toggling does not steal focus (no autofocus into the panel) — the player stays the non-modal <section aria-label="Video player"> (no dialog / aria-modal / trap / backdrop).
• The revealed panel is the dock's sole scroll area; the frame + bar stay pinned (shrink-0). Opening a panel grows the height reported by onDockMetrics (collapsed default reports a small height); the 88dvh cap + ≥12dvh article slice still hold.
• Curate reveal directions are real buttons with full aria-labels ("Curate this clip: {caption}" / "Dismiss as not relevant: {caption}"), matching #123.
